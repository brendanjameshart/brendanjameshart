## What is Here

Three open-access, interactive slide presentations for introductory and intermediate economics. Each is self-contained HTML with Chart.js diagrams and speaker notes — viewable in any browser. Additional repositories covering chart and report production tooling will be added over time.

### [Entry, Exit & Long-Run Profitability](https://brendanjameshart.github.io/pc-market-structures/)

Why economic profits are hard to keep, how free entry and exit drive profits to zero in competitive markets, and what strategies firms use to sustain profits through barriers to entry. Covers the U-shaped average cost curve, entry and exit progressions, three-case equilibrium analysis, and cost advantages. 34 slides.

→ [github.com/brendanjameshart/pc-market-structures](https://github.com/brendanjameshart/pc-market-structures)

### [Externalities & Public Goods](https://brendanjameshart.github.io/pc-externalities-public-goods/)

How markets fail when costs and benefits spill over to third parties, why public goods are systematically undersupplied, and what role government has in correcting these failures. Covers negative and positive externalities, the Coase theorem, public vs. private goods, and the logic of collective action. 40 slides.

→ [github.com/brendanjameshart/pc-externalities-public-goods](https://github.com/brendanjameshart/pc-externalities-public-goods)

### [The Federal Reserve, Monetary Policy & Money Creation](https://brendanjameshart.github.io/pc-fed-primer/)

How the U.S. central bank shapes the economy — from the mechanics of money creation to the tools the Fed uses to manage inflation and unemployment. Covers the money multiplier, the federal funds rate, open market operations, and the Fed's dual mandate.

→ [github.com/brendanjameshart/pc-fed-primer](https://github.com/brendanjameshart/pc-fed-primer)

## Stack

This profile hosts code and materials adjacent to that work: report production tooling, chart libraries, teaching resources, and reproducible research. Most published work is produced in Python with ReportLab for programmatic PDF builds, matplotlib for charts, and XeLaTeX with Pandoc for typeset reports. The design system uses Instrument Sans as the primary typeface, with a navy and coral palette applied across charts and report layouts. Code here is written to support that production workflow rather than as standalone software.
